Coding Guide

Quick reference for common ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS codes and their descriptions. This is a condensed version to give you a feel for how a guide works.

ICD-10 (Diagnosis Codes)

  • **I10**: Essential (primary) hypertension
  • **E11.9**: Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications
  • **J02.9**: Acute pharyngitis, unspecified
  • **R07.9**: Chest pain, unspecified

CPT (Procedure Codes)

  • **99214**: Established patient office visit, moderate complexity
  • **71045**: Radiologic examination, chest; single view, frontal
  • **45378**: Colonoscopy, flexible, diagnostic
  • **12002**: Simple repair of superficial wounds of scalp, neck, axillae, external genitalia, trunk and/or extremities

HCPCS (DME & Services)

  • **G8411**: Influenza virus vaccine, pediatric
  • **J3420**: Vitamin B-12 injection

HIPAA Guide

HIPAA, or the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, is a U.S. law that protects patient health information. It establishes standards for the security and privacy of this data. Key components include:

  • **Privacy Rule:** Governs the use and disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI).
  • **Security Rule:** Sets standards for securing PHI in electronic format.
  • **Breach Notification Rule:** Requires covered entities to notify affected individuals of a breach.

CMS 1500 Guide

The CMS-1500 is the standard paper claim form used by non-institutional providers to bill for professional services. It is used to submit claims to Medicare, Medicaid, and private insurance companies. It contains critical information such as:

  • Patient and Insured Information
  • Diagnosis and Procedure Codes
  • Service Date and Charge Information
